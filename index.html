3D Qualitative Segmentation Results

Abstract

The quantification of subcortical volume development from 3D fetal ultrasound can provide diagnostic information during routine pregnancy monitoring. However, manual segmentation of subcortical structures in ultrasound volumes is time-consuming and challenging due to low soft tissue contrast and speckle. For this reason, we propose to use a convolutional neural network (CNN) for the segmentation of the choroid plexus (CP), left posterior ventricle horn (LPVH), cavum septum pellucidum et vargae (CSPV) and cerebellum (CB) from 3D ultrasound. As ground-truth labels are scarce and expensive to obtain we explore few-shot learning, in which only a small number of manual annotations is used to train a CNN. We compare training a CNN using only a few individually annotated volumes versus using many weakly labelled volumes obtained from atlas-based labels. This showed that segmentation performance close to intra-observariability can be obtained with only a handful of manual annotations (Dice score of 0.86, 0.84, 0.80 and 0.90 for the CP, LPVH, CSPV and CB respectively versus an intra-observariabilty Dice score of 0.87, 0.85, 0.86, and 0.91). Finally, the trained models were applied to a large number of US volumes of a diverse, healthy population, obtaining novel US-specific growth curves of the respective structures during the second trimester of gestation.
